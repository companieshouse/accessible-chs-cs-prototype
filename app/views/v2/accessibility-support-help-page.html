Support to use our services

We can provide extra support if you need it to use our services. For example, if you:

  • have sensory disabilities, like a visual, hearing or speech impairment
  • have reduced mobility or physical disabilities
  • have dyslexia, autism or cognitive difficulties
  • experience seizures
  • have mental health conditions, like depression, stress or anxiety

We can discuss what support we can provide to help you meet your responsibilities. With your permission, we can also add a note to our internal system so we can support you in the future.

For example, if you would prefer us to contact you by phone, we can call to remind you when your confirmation statement or accounts are due.

How we can help

We can:

  • help you to complete online services or paper forms - a member of our customer support team will guide and support you through the process
  • provide alternative formats for paper forms
  • send you early reminders, or provide reminders in your preferred format
  • give you advice on speaking to a trusted friend or family member to get support
  • take calls through a video relay service, if you need to contact us using British Sign Language (BSL)

Advice and support from AbilityNet

AbilityNet offer technical advice and support for older people and disabled people of any age.

This includes advice on making your device easier to use if you have a disability - for example, changing the colours of a website and helpful ways to make your keyboard, mouse and mobile device easier to use. It also gives guidance on using voice recognition to speak to your device.

Read more about advice and support from AbilityNet.

Accessibility testing and user research

To help us make our services accessible and easy to use, our researchers work to identify and understand our users. This includes testing our services with people who have accessibility needs.

Help us to improve our services
